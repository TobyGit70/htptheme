2. Governing Laws and Regulations

2.1 Constitutional Amendment

Arkansas Medical Marijuana Amendment (Amendment 98)

Approved by Arkansas voters in November 2016, Amendment 98 establishes the framework for:

  • Medical marijuana cultivation
  • Processing and manufacturing
  • Dispensary operations
  • Patient and caregiver access

2.2 State Statutes

Arkansas Code Annotated ยง 20-63-101 et seq. (Arkansas Medical Marijuana Act)

State law implementing Amendment 98, governing:

  • Licensing requirements and procedures
  • Facility standards and security protocols
  • Record-keeping and reporting obligations
  • Product testing and quality standards
  • Transportation and distribution rules
  • Penalties for non-compliance

2.3 Regulatory Agencies

We operate under the oversight of:

  • Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration (DFA): License issuance and renewal
  • Arkansas Alcoholic Beverage Control Division (ABC): Enforcement and inspections

3. Compliance Programs

3.1 Seed-to-Sale Tracking

We utilize Arkansas's state-mandated tracking system to monitor:

  • Receipt of raw cannabis materials
  • Processing and manufacturing activities
  • Product inventory at all stages
  • Transfers to dispensaries
  • Waste disposal and destruction

Every product batch is tagged and tracked from receipt through final distribution.

3.2 Product Testing

All products undergo mandatory third-party testing by Arkansas-licensed laboratories for:

  • Potency Analysis: THC, CBD, CBN, and other cannabinoids
  • Contaminant Screening:
    • Pesticides and herbicides
    • Heavy metals (lead, arsenic, mercury, cadmium)
    • Residual solvents
    • Microbial contamination (E. coli, Salmonella, Aspergillus)
    • Mycotoxins (aflatoxins, ochratoxin)
  • Terpene Profiles: For product characterization

Products failing testing cannot be sold and must be remediated or destroyed per regulations.

3.3 Packaging and Labeling

All products comply with Arkansas packaging and labeling requirements:

  • Child-resistant packaging for all medical marijuana products
  • Opaque, resealable containers to maintain product integrity
  • Required label information:
    • Product name and batch number
    • THC and CBD content (mg and percentage)
    • Net weight or volume
    • Manufacturing and expiration dates
    • Processor license number
    • Dispensary information (when applicable)
    • Warning statements (pregnancy, impairment, age restrictions)
    • Ingredients and allergen information
  • Universal symbol indicating medical marijuana product

3.4 Security and Surveillance

Our facility maintains Arkansas-mandated security measures:

  • 24/7 video surveillance with 90-day retention
  • Controlled access systems with badge authentication
  • Intrusion detection and alarm systems
  • Visitor logs and escort protocols
  • Secure storage for all cannabis products
  • Perimeter fencing and lighting

3.5 Transportation

All product transportation complies with Arkansas regulations:

  • Licensed transportation services only
  • Secure vehicles with GPS tracking
  • Transport manifests for all shipments
  • Two-person minimum crew requirement
  • Direct routes with no stops
  • Real-time tracking in seed-to-sale system

4. Record Keeping

We maintain comprehensive records for minimum 7 years:

  • All business transactions and financial records
  • Inventory records (receipts, processing, sales, waste)
  • Product testing results and certificates of analysis
  • Transportation manifests and delivery confirmations
  • Employee training and background check records
  • Security footage and incident reports
  • Compliance inspections and remediation actions

5. Employee Requirements

All Happy Turtle employees must:

  • Be 21 years of age or older
  • Pass Arkansas State Police background checks
  • Obtain medical marijuana industry identification badges
  • Complete mandatory training programs including:
    • Arkansas cannabis laws and regulations
    • Product safety and quality control
    • Seed-to-sale tracking systems
    • Security protocols
    • Emergency procedures
  • Maintain current training certifications

6. Inspections and Audits

Happy Turtle is subject to unannounced inspections by:

  • Arkansas Alcoholic Beverage Control Division
  • Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration
  • Local law enforcement and fire marshals
  • State health inspectors

We maintain full cooperation with all regulatory inspections and audits, providing immediate access to:

  • All facility areas
  • Records and documentation
  • Inventory and products
  • Security systems and footage

7. Waste Disposal

All cannabis waste is handled according to Arkansas protocols:

  • Cannabis waste must be rendered unusable and unrecognizable
  • Mixed with non-consumable materials (soil, compost, grease)
  • Documented in seed-to-sale tracking system
  • Disposed within 72 hours of rendering unusable
  • Waste records maintained for 7 years

8. Product Recalls

We maintain a product recall plan including:

  • Rapid identification of affected batches
  • Immediate notification to dispensaries and regulators
  • Product retrieval and quarantine procedures
  • Investigation of root causes
  • Corrective action implementation
  • Documentation and reporting
