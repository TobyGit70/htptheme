2. Information We Collect

2.1 Business Information

From licensed business partners, we collect:

  • Arkansas medical marijuana license numbers and verification
  • Business name, DBA, and legal entity information
  • Contact information (business email, phone, address)
  • Authorized representative names and credentials
  • Transaction and order history
  • Product testing and compliance documentation

2.2 Regulatory Compliance Data

As required by Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration (DFA) and Arkansas Alcoholic Beverage Control Division (ABC), we maintain:

  • Seed-to-sale tracking data via Arkansas's medical marijuana tracking system
  • Product batch information and testing results
  • Chain of custody documentation
  • Transaction manifests and transport logs
  • Compliance reports and inspection records

3. How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

  • Verify Arkansas medical marijuana license status
  • Process wholesale orders and manufacturing requests
  • Comply with Arkansas medical marijuana regulations
  • Maintain accurate seed-to-sale tracking records
  • Conduct required product testing and quality control
  • Generate compliance reports for state regulators
  • Communicate about orders, products, and regulatory updates
  • Maintain business records as required by law

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

4.1 Regulatory Authorities

We share information with Arkansas regulatory authorities as required:

  • Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration (DFA)
  • Arkansas Alcoholic Beverage Control Division (ABC)
  • Arkansas Department of Health (ADH)
  • Law enforcement agencies upon lawful request

4.2 Third-Party Service Providers

We may share information with:

  • Licensed third-party testing laboratories
  • Compliance software and seed-to-sale tracking providers
  • Secure transportation services (licensed only)
  • Legal and accounting professionals bound by confidentiality

5. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect business information:

  • Secure, encrypted data transmission (SSL/TLS)
  • Access controls and authentication systems
  • Regular security audits and monitoring
  • Secure physical facilities with surveillance and access logs
  • Employee training on data security and confidentiality

6. Data Retention

We retain business and compliance records in accordance with Arkansas law:

  • Transaction records: Minimum 7 years (per Arkansas regulations)
  • Compliance documentation: Minimum 7 years
  • Product testing results: Minimum 7 years
  • Seed-to-sale tracking data: As required by state systems

7. Your Rights

As a licensed business partner, you have the right to:

  • Access your business information in our records
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Receive transaction records and documentation
  • Withdraw consent where applicable (subject to legal requirements)

Note: We cannot delete information required for regulatory compliance or legal record-keeping.
